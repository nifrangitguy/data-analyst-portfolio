IBM Stock Forecasting with Prophet

This project applies advanced time series forecasting on IBM’s historical stock closing prices using the Prophet library. It includes two core analyses:

  1. Long-Term Monthly Forecasting (10 years)
  2. Short-Term Daily Forecasting with Weekly Seasonality (6 months)

Together, these models uncover both macro-level trends and micro-level seasonal patterns in IBM’s stock behavior.

1. Monthly Forecast – Long-Term Trend Analysis

The data was first resampled into monthly averages, and a 10-year forecast was generated using Prophet's default settings.

  • Converted historical daily data into monthly closing averages using .resample("ME").mean().
  • Trained a Prophet model on this aggregated data.
  • Forecasted the next 120 months (10 years) of closing price behavior.
  • Merged forecast with historical data for direct comparison.
  • Used .plot_components() to break down forecasted components.

Insights:

  • The trend component captures IBM’s overall growth or decline trajectory.
  • The forecast chart with confidence intervals shows increasing uncertainty over time.
  • Ideal for investors and planners wanting a macro view of long-term stock movement.

2. Daily Forecast – Weekly Seasonality Analysis

To analyze short-term volatility, the data was modeled using daily frequency with explicit weekly seasonality enabled.

  • Used Prophet(weekly_seasonality=True) to capture weekly cyclic patterns.
  • Forecasted the next 180 days of closing prices.
  • Visualized both the full forecast and component breakdown (trend + weekly).

Insights:

  • The weekly component reveals recurring patterns by day of week.
  • Helpful for short-term decisions like trading strategies or risk assessment.

Combined Understanding:

  • Monthly forecast = long-range outlook
  • Daily forecast = short-term fluctuation visibility
  • .plot_components() isolates trend, seasonality, holidays/events

Portfolio Summary: “Using Prophet, I applied both long-term and short-term forecasting to IBM’s stock data. I first resampled the data monthly to forecast 10 years into the future, revealing overall trend movements and confidence intervals. Then, I performed a daily forecast with explicit weekly seasonality to uncover short-term behavioral patterns. Component breakdowns helped isolate trend and seasonality, giving business stakeholders both strategic and tactical insights.”
